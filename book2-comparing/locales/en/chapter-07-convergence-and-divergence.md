# Chapter 7: Convergence, divergence, and where the harnesses agree

This chapter ties the two paths together. Both Claude Code and Codex agree on one thing: models are untrustworthy. Their convergence is thus philosophical—they both build harnesses with prompt layering, tool policy, state handling, recovery, verification, and local governance. Their divergence lies in placement: Claude Code invests in runtime flexibility and on-the-fly governance; Codex invests in explicit structural control.

Teams that feel their long sessions get derailed will appreciate Claude Code’s focus on continuity, recovery, and dynamic intervention. Teams that cannot clearly describe rule states will gravitate toward Codex’s typed fragments, approval policy, and thread management.

The real value in comparison is recognizing that different uncertainties call for different harness architectures. Where you place control determines where the system grows its discipline.
